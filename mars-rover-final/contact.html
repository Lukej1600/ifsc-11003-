background

The history of Mars exploration is defined by a series of increasingly sophisticated robotic pioneers that have fundamentally changed our understanding of the Red Planet. This ambitious journey began in 1997 with Sojourner, a modest proof-of-concept that paved the way for the more robust "twin" rovers, Spirit and Opportunity.

As technology advanced, NASA transitioned to larger, nuclear-powered laboratories like Curiosity and, most recently, Perseverance. By traversing rugged Martian landscapes and analyzing soil samples in situ, these missions have provided critical insights into the planet’s ancient geology and shifting climate. More importantly, they continue to search for the chemical signatures that might prove Mars was once capable of supporting microbial life.
